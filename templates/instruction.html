Thank you for installing EcoCart!

Next, we’ll walk you through getting EcoCart set up into your store. Don’t worry, it only takes about 8 minutes.

If you’re more of a video person, you will find our install video guide here.

EcoCart is a Shopify Partner and can assist you with the setup, customization, or placement at any time. For help or any other questions, please contact us at support@ecocart.io.

1. Account Settings

First, fill out some basic information about your shop in the Account Settings Tab.

Then, select who will pay for the offsets:

  • 1. Customers: Give your customers the option to make their orders carbon neutral.
  • 2. Company: Make all your products carbon neutral by paying for the offsets on behalf of your customers. (You can change this setting at any time and the change happens instantly!)

2. Click save and you will be prompted to EcoCart.io to create an account

3. Create your EcoCart Account

Fill out your company name, email, and password to create your account. (Your shop URL will be autofilled)

4. Billing Information

In the Billing tab of your EcoCart dashboard, fill out your billing information. Don’t worry, EcoCart is still completely free for you, we just need you to collect the carbon offset fees on our behalf.

5. Back to Shopify Dashboard

After hitting ‘Add Your Card,’ click the link in the pop-up box to be redirected back to your EcoCart Shopify Dashboard.

6. Placement on the Page

EcoCart auto-integrates with your shop if you’re using any of the following themes:

  • Debut
  • Supply
  • Brooklyn
  • Boundless
  • Simple
  • Venture
  • Minimal

If you ARE using one of these themes, you can skip this step completely!

If you’re using any other themes, or if you want EcoCart to show up anywhere other than the placement of the auto-integration, you can do this with a little HTML magic. We’ll walk you through that below.

First, figure out where in your cart you’d like EcoCart to be placed. For example, this store first decided that they would like EcoCart to appear above the CHECKOUT button in their store.

To do this, right-click on the checkout button and choose Inspect.

It's important that you choose a class that is unique; otherwise, EcoCart could appear in multiple places throughout the page. You would not want to choose the class Button, for example, because a lot of elements on the page probably use this class.

HELPFUL TIP: If the class that you choose happens to be a two-part class name that contains a space like “cart__shipping rte” then all you have to do is remove everything after the space for it to work correctly: “cart__shipping”. Don’t forget to double check that the first part of this class is unique on the page.

Once you have decided on a class, copy and paste it into your Customization Settings under “Placement.” In this case, we want the EcoCart box to appear above the element with the class Cart__Checkout, so we selected the “before” placement option.

Make sure to include a period (.) before any class name, and a hash (#) before any id, following standard CSS notation.

7. Design customization

Now you get to choose how EcoCart will look within your store. Below you can see how EcoCart looks by default.

If this works for you, simply click “Enable EcoCart” in the Account Settings tab and you’ll be all set up!

HELPFUL TIP: If the checkout info of your cart page is condensed over to one side, then you could end up with EcoCart looking squished like this:

To solve for this, we’ve created some design options to choose from for your “condensed cart” here in this doc.

All you have to do is copy and paste the code for each respective view into the code boxes of your Customization Settings tab of your Shopify dashboard.

If you want to customize the box, click “No” and HTML & CSS boxes will appear for your complete customization.

    There are 3 different widget preview boxes:
  • 1. Loading View: how the box looks while calculating the cost of making the order carbon neutral
  • 2. Website View: when displaying the carbon neutral order amount
  • 3. Success View: when a customer has checked the box to make their order carbon neutral.

If you are familiar with HTML & CSS, this should all make sense to you. If you have no idea what this all means, we’re happy to help. Just email support@ecocart.io and we will custom code the box to your specifications.

8. Enable EcoCart

Navigate back to the Account Settings tab of your EcoCart Shopify Dashboard and check the box ‘Enable EcoCart.’

That’s it! Now you’ll be able to offer carbon neutral products to your customers.

Helpful Tip: Hide the EcoCart Product

As you might have noticed, the EcoCart carbon neutral “product” automatically shows in the catalog of your store like this:

If you don’t want the carbon neutral “product” in your catalog, follow these three easy steps:

To prevent this from happening, follow these three easy steps:

  • 1. In the settings of your shop, go to Products > Collections, then click “Create Collection” with the title ‘All’ (if you have a collection titled ‘All’ already, then skip this step)
  • 2. Create the following rules for this collection:
    a. Product Price is greater than 0 b. Product Title is not equal to Carbon Neutral Order
  • 3. Then go to Products > Carbon Neutral Order > Search Engine Listing Preview and remove the EcoCart’s SEO URL by unchecking the “Create a URL redirect for…” checkbox that appears only after clicking into the ‘URL and handle’ box.

That’s it - you’re all done. Your customers can now make their orders carbon neutral when they shop with you!
